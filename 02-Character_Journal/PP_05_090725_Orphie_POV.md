# Orphie's Character Journal

## Session 05 — Do You Smell That? It's Breakfast Time (09/07/2025)

> Pasted verbatim from the Session 05 POV Overview (Convo 1). Voice-dependent expansion is done separately in chat, not here.

We flew into a graveyard of our enemies. Selena had been here, and you could read it in the field, hundreds of the dead stacked and skewered, five of them run through on a single bone arrow like a grim little kabob. I was on the fence about her people before. I'm convinced now. We dug our way into the bone-stink and the purple murk and found her on the ground, breathing wrong, something black crawling under her skin like it owned her. So we did the only thing this family knows how to do: we threw everything we had into a needle. Ugin's fire, the mushroom king's strange medicine, Rin's borrowed light, my brother's blood, even my spit, gods help her, and we put it all into her and told her to rise. She woke with white eyes and asked to be taken deeper, and we did that too, because you don't get to save someone halfway.

And then the man who took my dog-grieving day from me, the smug metal-handed schemer from the tower, was standing in the pits poisoning the well that made her. The one who tortured Chandra. The one who's been carrying off the Sun. He stole a piece of my essence, twice, like that was something he was allowed to do. So I jumped the pits with my lungs burning and I hit him, and hit him, and his wounds kept closing like he was laughing at me. I asked for help and meant it. And then, finally, after a whole stretch of feeling like the small one in a story full of giants, the axe answered. Two perfect strikes, one after the other, and I put Athena's fire clean through his neck and told him it was breakfast time. His head hit the ground still talking. "The harvest is coming."

We pushed Selena into the pit she was born from, because she wanted it and because we're done deciding her life for her. The whole place came down around us while she cooked in that light, and we stayed, every one of us, even when the ceiling tried to bury us for it. We don't abandon her again. We dragged ourselves out into the open air with a god's last threat ringing in my ears and one of our own still under the surface, becoming something. Whatever's standing over that mushroom king now, white-eyed in the dark, it had better remember whose family it belongs to.
