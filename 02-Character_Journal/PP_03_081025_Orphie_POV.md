# Orphie's Character Journal

## Session 03 — That's Why No One Will Remember Your Name (08/10/2025)

### THAT'S WHY NO ONE WILL REMEMBER YOUR NAME: ORPHIE'S ACCOUNT

We woke up to the whole world screaming. Not figuratively. The city was tearing itself apart on every side — one army crashing down from the north, a dead tide rising up from the south — and there was no version of the morning where we got to stay in bed. So we split. Ogre and Varis went where the fighting was loudest. Rin, Sanis and I went the other way, and then everything I'd thrown us out of the night before came roaring back, and a thing the size of a building put an arrow through the sky and swatted us down like we were nothing.

When I came to, my first job was the only job I ever really trust myself with: I got everyone off the wreck, made them stop, made them check. Everybody, all limbs attached. We've been doing this long enough that I know the panic comes later. First you count heads. Sanis had cookies, of all things, hoarded like a squirrel. We laughed. It had been so long since anything was that small and that kind.

Then the sky split open over the dead district and a dragon the size of a nightmare walked out and announced he'd come to judge us all. I want it on record that I am very tired of men deciding they get to be the one who decides. My parents were like that. This one was worse, and bigger, and he had a voice that got into your teeth.

The thing about being me, in a fight like this, is that I am exactly one woman with an axe. Everyone around me was throwing fire and turning into monsters and I kept asking — beg me to be bigger, give me a reason to be close enough to matter — and nobody could. So I drove. I put my people on the back of a half-dead mechanical horse and I cut a road through the dead so the ones who could do the impossible would get their chance. That's a kind of useful. It's just not the kind that feels like anything.

Chandra came back. Out of nowhere, out of years, thinner and harder and burning at the edges, and I called her a name and meant it like a hug. Of course it was her. It's always the people you stopped expecting.

And then — finally — the god went down. They wore it out, all of them at once, pinned it and burned it and wrapped it in everything we had, and when it hit the ground at our level I climbed up its leg and I carved my name into it. Small. So it'd know. So somebody would. I've spent this whole stretch feeling like furniture in my own story and I was not going to let a god die without leaving a mark that said I was here.

The other dragon, the one who actually liked us — they flew up to fight the judge, and they lost. I watched a thing that called this plane its own get folded into a black nothing and come out as bones. And they sent Varis a message on the way out telling us not to come. Don't be reckless. Wait. I'm so sick of dragons sacrificing themselves and telling us it's fine. It is never fine. I told Molly that no one would remember her name for falling back, and I knew even as I said it that I was really talking about all of us — about whether any of this leaves a scratch on the world or just gets swallowed like the rest.

I wanted to quit. I said something ugly about existence and Rin caught it before it could land, the way she does. And then something flicked me right between the eyes, hard, the way only one person ever did, and I heard her plain as day: survive, you idiot. So I will. Grandmothers don't ask twice.

We carried Chandra to the tree where the healers are. She'd been caged and starved and clawed for years for the crime of trying to save children, and she still tried to wave us off like it was nothing. She told us how they're stealing the spark out of people — how they kill them first and pull the light out after, with a spell and a spear. And just as we were catching our breath, a nervous girl with daggers and mushrooms in her hair pressed a bone into Sanis's hand and said his sister is hurt and waiting. So. No rest. There's never any rest. But I'm still standing, and I've got a name carved into a god to prove I was here, and that'll have to be enough for tonight.
