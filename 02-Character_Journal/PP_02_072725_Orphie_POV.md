# Orphie's Character Journal

## Session 02 — I Can't Rebuild You (07/27/2025)

### I CAN'T REBUILD YOU: ORPHIE'S ACCOUNT

We were still standing on top of the Izzet tower when I decided I'd had enough of the man who'd dragged the whole multiverse to our doorstep without asking a single soul. Ral kept cackling in front of that beacon, proud as anything, talking like summoning every planeswalker alive was a favor he'd done the world. So I gave him a lesson on consent. I asked if I could hit him. He said no. I hit him anyway. That's the point, I told him — you asked, they said no, and you did it anyway. He still didn't get it, but my fist did. I used my last rage on it and I'd do it again.

Ogre kept tossing that cursed ball around like it was a toy at recess, and Rin finally snatched it out of the air before anyone else got their brain scrambled. We learned the souls he'd vacuumed up weren't gone — they were all still in there, every last one. Kaya thinks she destroyed them. She didn't. We did. That's a secret worth keeping.

Down in Dalya's lab, Ogre managed to fuse that ball straight into Pip's little game box. The whole thing turned black and went quiet, Pip's face frozen in a shocked little flicker before the screen died. Dalya wasn't even mad — she'd already made a copy. But the copy's wrong. Rin could tell, and so could I in my own way: it talks like Pip but there's nothing behind the eyes. No soul. We agreed not to let Dalya go hunting for one to stuff inside it. Some doors you don't open.

Sanis came back from his own head that night looking sharper, steadier, like the thing living in him had finally stopped fighting and started helping. He carries his ghost a little better than the rest of us carry ours. We took the long rest we'd all been begging for, in our own apartment, with Clarissa fussing about her bar shift like the sky wasn't about to fall.

It fell anyway. We woke to fire on the horizon and a black-and-blue tide chewing through the city. The dead were marching — metal-plated things they're calling Eternals — and the Gruul picked that exact morning to turn on us. Molly at the Boros hall looked like she wanted to quit and couldn't. So we split up. We sent the big guns ahead — Steve grown to the size of a district, Varis turned dragon and bigger than that — to grind the dead into the dirt, while the rest of us went looking for the people who might actually know something. We found Nissa smoking herself stupid in a tent, telling me to relax while the world burned. No word on Chandra. No word on Gideon. Everyone tucked so deep into their own corner they've forgotten there's a fight outside it. I was so tired of all of them.

Then the ground screamed and a pyramid clawed its way up out of the south, a white light burning at its peak. Rin dropped to her knees when she understood what it was. The lights crawling toward it — those were sparks. Planeswalker sparks, getting pulled out of people and gathered up like coins. We hadn't trapped them here to save them. Someone wanted them all in one place so they could be harvested. We'd helped set the table.

We flew for that pyramid, and a god stood up to meet us. Sixty feet of it, a bow in its hands, and it had us in its sights. Sanis took the shot of his life — I've never seen him stand that straight — and white fire bloomed where his arrow struck. For one breath it was beautiful. Then the god turned its eyes on us, and the next arrow tore the tavern open like paper. Rin nearly died right there beside me.

So I made the only choice that mattered. I grabbed Sanis, I screamed at Rin to fly, and I cut the tavern loose and let it fall. Ogre would've cried over it once. Not me, not today. I told them the truth as we dropped through the smoke with the ground hiding somewhere below: we can build another tavern. We have the parts, we have the time, we'll make it better. But I can't rebuild you. None of you. As long as we hit the ground breathing, I don't give a damn about the rest.

We're falling now, blind, holding onto each other and a half-dead metal horse. Together. That's the part I'm keeping.
