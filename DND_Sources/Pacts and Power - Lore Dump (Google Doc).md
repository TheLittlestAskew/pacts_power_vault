**Introduction**

  

In the infinite space of the multiverse, Power is king. Power dictates the flow of magic and as a tool, allows the bending of fate itself. With enough Power, even time and the threads of reality are but clay, to be formed in the hands of the beholder. Luckily, in most cases, this Power is confined to the very essence of planes themselves.

  

On the plane of Arcavios, this Power manifests itself in the form of Five Founding Dragons, children of the snarl. Strixhaven students studying magic channel the magic of these dragons, and consequently, the very heart of the plane. On the plane of Ravnica, this Power manifests itself in intricate leylines sprawled across the globe, lending to its apt name, the Implicit Maze. Those who would use Power for good would strike a chord with the first ten Guildleaders of Ravnica. As founders of the Guildpact, the Guildleaders forged a Pact that bound the plane to the principles of peace and prosperity.

  

Now, while some may view peace and prosperity as “good”, there are those that view these ideals as “weak” and “defective”. These ideals, while perfectly acceptable to possess, are dangerous in the wrong hands, but especially in the hands of planeswalkers. Namely, a very ancient and especially wicked planeswalker. 

  

What happens when a planeswalker collects Power on a planar scale? What happens when a planeswalker collects Power on an interplanar scale? Such tales have not been told since the Times of Legends, a time when all planeswalkers beheld such power. This time was a time of great peace, prosperity, and technological innovation. Yet, as is common with sentient life and possession of Overwhelming Power, this Time culminated in the Dark Age and utter loss. Now, almost ten thousand years later, we may see the pages of time once again tell the same story.

  

The Breakfast Club finds themselves coming to in a cave on the plane of Ravnica. This was a routine journey and retrieve quest for a local townsperson that should not have ended in a total group knockout. How could this happen? What the group does not remember, is that they were not on a retrieval quest at all. Just moments before, they helped reknit the threads of reality to tell a different tale. A tale to delay an ancient and wicked planeswalker from taking over the multiverse. An admirable effort, to be sure, but one that only delayed the inevitable.

  

Unbeknownst to them, machinations move gears in the background. Plots endanger their unknowingly adoptive home of Ravnica. The state of their original plane of Arcadios is unknown. How do the brave adventurers of the breakfast club spin their rewritten tale?

**Theme(s):**

  

 The beginning of every story starts with another story’s end. This does not mean that this other story has ended in vain; its conclusion is what may set the stage for a new tale. Though magical power may ebb and flow, the fabric of time is endless. Time has and always will march on.

 We open on the plane of Strixhaven. Strixhaven, renowned as a place of magical learning and schooling for the magically inclined, is the adopted home of a certain group of adventurers.

**Session 1: The Breakfast Club**

  

We open the first session of Pacts and Power where the group last left off, in the mouth of a cavern at Strixhaven University. Unbeknownst to them, their very realities were about to change forever.

  

  - The party was immediately greeted by a dark robed magician, whose ancient and evil presence gave a dastardly impression. The dark magician, wearing a levitating, opal gem above his head, was accompanied by a brown robed artificer with a latticed black and red right arm and Professor Onyx, an enigmatic teacher-sorcerer at Strixhaven.
  - The Dark Magician then tethered the party to a mysterious crystal staff through glowing, yellow threads. One by one, he began to untie the knots that littered the threads, slowly unbinding the party from history.
  - After the party passed out, the five dragon paragons of Strixhaven met with the party in a dream. With the dragons’ help, the party broke free of the tethers and re-knotted their threads, but doing so bound them to a different plane and reality.
  - A short existential experience later, the party’s members were pulled downwards, with bubbles rushing past, opaque at first, then becoming transparent. These memory bubbles began playing the party members’ pivotal, coming of age moments in real time. 
  - **Lance** experienced his father picking him up and swinging him around, a playful and light memory - one of the only times Lance’s father ever smiled. 
      
      - The next memory that played saw Lance arriving home after school. Two Dimir agents had his father restrained and questioning about “shipments”. Lance managed to deal a severe knife throw to one of the agent’s legs, but did not stop them from permanently enfeebling his father and mutilating his father’s knee. The Dimir agents then made their escape and left a crestfallen Lance to pick up the pieces.
  - The next memory saw **Rin** playing in her family farm’s field with her childhood best friend, Penny. After a moment, Rin’s mom called the pair in for the Genleth farm’s special hot and sweet milk tea and snacks. This memory is filled with warmth, not just from the hot drinks and food, but also from friends and family.
      
      - Fast forward to Rin at fifteen - with Penny absent, a trio of bullies call Rin “Shrimpy” and jump Rin after school. Bruised and traumatized, Rin runs to Granny’s Books, her sanctuary in the nearby town of Pern. Granny embraces Rin and magically heals her injuries.
  - **Ogre** felt like it was time for another game. A game that he felt he had played before. This time, Ogre was in the forest playing a game called “Stick” with his dad, Rod, a forest halfling. After a good go, Rod and Ogre walked back to Rod’s bucolic abode and made a game of separating mushrooms they harvested earlier that day - a peaceful, idyllic memory.
      
      - The next memory saw Rod taking Ogre to the forest Halfling temple. Rod had a conversation with the temple priests, while Ogre played a game called “Sit and Wait”. Afterwards, Rod told Ogre that it was time for another game, a game where Ogre was “needed.” The temple head priest performed a teleportation ritual and sent a naive Ogre to another plane, while Rod stood silent, nose running and tears dripping. Ogre popped out in Ravnica and immediately befriended a nearby banana stand vendor.
  - **Orphie** found herself in boarding school back in Cania. While outside was cold and snowing, she reached for chips and dip and discovered she had no more. Orphie’s best friend Clarissa then walked in with steaming, spiced soup, and the two enjoyed a moment of warmth. After Clarissa confessed that she had a bully in her class, Orphie swore to “take care of it”.
      
      - The next day saw Orphie confronting Clarissa’s bully Gina. Without skipping a beat, Orphie took out her heaviest book, a hardcover copy of “War and Peace” and thwacked Gina in the back of the head. Gina retaliated by pushing Orphie to the ground. In a kairotic moment of deciding between her spellbook and a heavy stapler gifted by Clarissa, Orphie grasped the dense metal fastener and clocked the bully in the face, knocking her into a row of desks. 
      - The next scene opened to Orphie’s parents, specifically her father, scolding Orphie, not because violence is bad, but for using brute force, instead of magic. Magical means have and always will be the family way to deal with issues, NOT physical means.
  - **Varis** does not remember this memory, but he was left on a certain elven Orzhov priest’s front steps. After a quick squeeze, this soon to be father took the baby Varis inside. Fast forward to middle school, Varis enjoyed a chill afternoon listening to some Metal Maiden records with his best friend Ben.
      
      - After Guildball practice a couple weeks later, Varis is kidnapped on his way to the locker room. Blindfolded and gagged, Varis listens to his kidnappers stumble about what to do. The conversation moves to “harvesting” and “getting the job done”. Right as Varis’s arm is grabbed, sounds of struggle and fighting happen all around him. The first thing Varis sees is his father, having brought the Orzhov to save his adopted son. If it weren’t for Ben tipping the rescuers off, Varis might not have lived to see another day.
  - **Sanis** smelled the sweet scents of funnel cake and cotton candy as a memory played out with his sister Salena’s first Ravnican Spring Festival. The pair rode on an Izzet Ferris wheel, as Sanis pointed out all the famous landmarks of the Tenth District. He would take Salena to see some of them the next day.
      
      - Fast forward to Sanis waiting for his father to come home. His mom Malena, sipping wine and reading a book on the couch barely acknowledged his presence. As soon as Sanis turned to go back to his room, his father came home, family longbow strapped across his chest. Made of the finest Oak and reinforced by Drow magic, the indestructible longbow had been passed down the Reylana family for generations. 
      - After downing a bottle of wine, Sanis’s father showed Sanis how to draw and shoot a bow, while Salena eagerly watched. Hours passed by before Sanis made his well-earned first hit, prompting Sanis to ask his father if he knew anything special about the day. Sadly, his father answered no, utterly clueless. Sanis, visibly upset, went inside, but not before Salena presented him with a birthday cupcake.
  - The next memory saw the party’s first encounter together. “The Breakfast Club” met in the library when some were serving detention and others were just hanging out. Orphie and Varis physically defended their friends but were in trouble since they were the ones seen throwing the punches at their respective bullies. Ogre was in trouble for not paying attention in class. Lance was caught illegally selling things at school. Rin was helping out the librarian, organizing shelves. Sanis was at a nearby table, not at detention, but had nowhere else to be.

After some of the party had taken damage, Rin suffered a meltdown and retaliated against the hag, incinerating it. From that moment on, “The Breakfast Club” were friends. The group graduated from high school and joined Ogre’s traveling tavern, fully equipped with an Airstream-like bar cart pulled by an Izzet robot horse named Scrappy.
  - The last bubble that played out before the party woke up depicted their old home of Strixhaven. In a cavern, dominated by the light of the plane’s primordial roil, the black magician (whom the party no longer remember) stood menacingly over five humanoids robed in the following colors: black/white, red/blue, black/green, red/white, and blue/green. The point of view zooms out to reveal the entire campus of Strixhaven University, its students all cloaked in the same black robes as the dark magician.
  - The party wakes up in a Golgari cave, a farmer’s tools recovered.
