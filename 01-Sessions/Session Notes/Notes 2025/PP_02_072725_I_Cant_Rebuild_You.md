## Section 2 — Orphie's POV Overview

### I CAN'T REBUILD YOU: ORPHIE'S ACCOUNT

We were still standing on top of the Izzet tower when I decided I'd had enough of the man who'd dragged the whole multiverse to our doorstep without asking a single soul. [[Ral Zarek|Ral]] kept cackling in front of that beacon, proud as anything, talking like summoning every planeswalker alive was a favor he'd done the world. So I gave him a lesson on consent. I asked if I could hit him. He said no. I hit him anyway. That's the point, I told him — you asked, they said no, and you did it anyway. He still didn't get it, but my fist did. I used my last rage on it and I'd do it again.

[[Ogre]] kept tossing that cursed ball around like it was a toy at recess, and [[Rin]] finally snatched it out of the air before anyone else got their brain scrambled. We learned the souls he'd vacuumed up weren't gone — they were all still in there, every last one. [[Kaya Cassir|Kaya]] thinks she destroyed them. She didn't. We did. That's a secret worth keeping.

Down in [[Dalya Bara|Dalya]]'s lab, Ogre managed to fuse that ball straight into Pip's little game box. The whole thing turned black and went quiet, Pip's face frozen in a shocked little flicker before the screen died. Dalya wasn't even mad — she'd already made a copy. But the copy's wrong. [[Rin]] could tell, and so could I in my own way: it talks like Pip but there's nothing behind the eyes. No soul. We agreed not to let Dalya go hunting for one to stuff inside it. Some doors you don't open.

[[Sanis]] came back from his own head that night looking sharper, steadier, like the thing living in him had finally stopped fighting and started helping. He carries his ghost a little better than the rest of us carry ours. We took the long rest we'd all been begging for, in our own apartment, with [[Clarissa]] fussing about her bar shift like the sky wasn't about to fall.

It fell anyway. We woke to fire on the horizon and a black-and-blue tide chewing through the city. The dead were marching — metal-plated things they're calling Eternals — and the [[Gruul Clans|Gruul]] picked that exact morning to turn on us. [[Molly]] at the Boros hall looked like she wanted to quit and couldn't. So we split up. We sent the big guns ahead — [[Steve]] grown to the size of a district, [[Varis]] turned dragon and bigger than that — to grind the dead into the dirt, while the rest of us went looking for the people who might actually know something. We found [[Nissa]] smoking herself stupid in a tent, telling me to relax while the world burned. No word on [[Chandra]]. No word on [[Gideon]]. Everyone tucked so deep into their own corner they've forgotten there's a fight outside it. I was so tired of all of them.

Then the ground screamed and a pyramid clawed its way up out of the south, a white light burning at its peak. Rin dropped to her knees when she understood what it was. The lights crawling toward it — those were sparks. Planeswalker sparks, getting pulled out of people and gathered up like coins. We hadn't trapped them here to save them. Someone wanted them all in one place so they could be harvested. We'd helped set the table.

We flew for that pyramid, and a god stood up to meet us. Sixty feet of it, a bow in its hands, and it had us in its sights. [[Sanis]] took the shot of his life — I've never seen him stand that straight — and white fire bloomed where his arrow struck. For one breath it was beautiful. Then the god turned its eyes on us, and the next arrow tore the [[Ogre's Tavern|tavern]] open like paper. Rin nearly died right there beside me.

So I made the only choice that mattered. I grabbed Sanis, I screamed at Rin to fly, and I cut the tavern loose and let it fall. Ogre would've cried over it once. Not me, not today. I told them the truth as we dropped through the smoke with the ground hiding somewhere below: we can build another tavern. We have the parts, we have the time, we'll make it better. But I can't rebuild you. None of you. As long as we hit the ground breathing, I don't give a damn about the rest.

We're falling now, blind, holding onto each other and a half-dead metal horse. Together. That's the part I'm keeping.

## Section 3 — Session Analysis

### Narrative Summary

The session unfolds in two halves on the same play date: a quieter aftermath sequence (Part 1) and a major battle (Part 2).

**Aftermath at Nivix — The Souls in the Ball (Part 1):** Picking up atop the [[Fortress of Nivix]] immediately after Session 01, the party debates [[Kaya Cassir|Kaya]]'s true motive for wanting the [[Ghost Mind]] destroyed (overwhelm/contract liberation vs information). [[Ogre]] throws the [[Leather Daddy Ball]] (LDB) repeatedly; [[Rin]] eventually catches it (WIS save 19) and confirms the absorbed [[Ghost Mind]] souls are intact and contained — and that the ball is nowhere near capacity. Kaya believes the spirits are destroyed; the party privately knows otherwise. They resolve to seek out planeswalkers they haven't seen in a while ([[Chandra]], [[Gideon]]) and to investigate the "second sun" thread and the [[Guildpact]] question raised in the Ghost Mind.

**Confronting Ral / The Consent Lesson (Part 1):** [[Ral Zarek|Ral]] remains by the [[Interplanar Beacon]], delighted with himself and oblivious that the [[Ghost Mind]]'s destruction (not his daily switch-flipping) enabled it. Rin attempts an Insight check (nat 1) to read him. Frustrated by Ral's non-consensual summoning of all planeswalkers, [[Orphie]] asks to punch him, is refused, and decks him anyway (16 to hit) to illustrate consent. The party debates who could replace Ral; [[Niv-Mizzet]] (a dragon, currently absent and not a planeswalker) is named as the rightful authority.

**Dalya's Lab & the LDGB Accident (Part 1):** Descending to [[Dalya Bara|Dalya]]'s laboratory, the party collects the cloned [[Pippi]] (Game Boy cartridge). Ogre brings the LDB into contact with Pip's Game Boy (straight d20 = 5); the LDB merges into the console, turning it black and translucent with visible spirits in the circuitry — the **Leather Daddy Game Boy (LDGB)** — and renders Pip unconscious. Rin's Arcana 23 reveals the [[Ghost Mind]] spirits plus the ball's pre-existing **death magic** fused with Pip's prototype spirit; the new entity needs time to recover. Dalya reveals she pre-emptively made a second Pip clone, and has already installed one copy in **[[EMLA]]** (a large Izzet mech/tank-beetle weapon). Rin and Varis privately determine the spare clone is soulless (Arcana/Religion 18) and agree to keep that from Dalya. Orphie tries to claim EMLA; Dalya refuses (it's Izzet property and unfinished). The party also confirms [[Pip|Pip]] is independent of the Ghost Mind and unaffected by its destruction.

**The Long Rest & Sanis's Power-Up (Part 1→2):** The party returns to [[Orphie]] and [[Rin]]'s Sunhome apartment (passing the parked [[Ogre's Tavern]]), greets roommate [[Clarissa]], and takes a long rest. [[Sanis]] wakes with his week-long headache gone. In his mind palace, a more "put-together" [[Szadek]] explains that by reuniting him, Sanis has become slightly more powerful: greater empathy, thicker mental walls, and the spell **Detect Thoughts** (one cast), with one additional effect still to be determined by the DM.

**War Reignites (Part 2):** Waking the next morning, the party sees from their tower window that the [[The Second Sun|Second Sun]]'s forces — black-and-blue metal-plated **[[Eternals]]** — are destroying buildings and advancing across the DMZ, while the [[Gruul Clans|Gruul]] advance from the north. The [[Boros Legion]]'s [[Parhelion II]] mobilizes. At Boros HQ they meet [[Molly]], who briefs them: the Eternals appeared overnight, come continuously from across the border, and her [[House Dimir|Dimir]] intelligence contacts have gone silent (a consequence of the party's Ghost Mind destruction — Rin admits it). No word on the [[Gatewatch]], Chandra, or Gideon.

**The Party Splits — Operation Ogre-Sized & Operation Celery (Part 2):** The party divides. **Team Ogre-Sized** ([[Varis]] in dragon form + [[Ogre]] + an enlarged [[Steve]]) heads to the front to thin the Eternal horde. **Team Celery** ([[Orphie]], [[Rin]], [[Sanis]]) takes the [[Ogre's Tavern|tavern]] to the [[Selesnya Conclave]] to seek the [[Gatewatch]]. Varis casts **Flame Arrows** on Sanis's quiver (next 12 arrows +1d6 white Ugin fire, 1 hour). Ogre gives Rin his **ever-smoking bottle** (no known command word).

**Operation Ogre-Sized (Part 2):** Varis (dragon) carries Ogre to the front; Ogre breaks **Steve's tooth** to summon Steve and feeds him/Varis **enlarge mushrooms** (~1 hour duration). Steve erupts from a tunnel, devouring Eternals (3 on emergence; 4d8 = 18 → 4 more). Varis breathes fire (18d6 = **58 force**, 14 Eternals incinerated), then later body-slams the field (**nat 20**, ~30 Eternals flattened) and uses a bite/claw multiattack (~14 more) with **Frightful Presence**, frightening the area. Ogre identifies the "funny-looking people" as planeswalkers being struck by spark-extracting **bidents** [transcribed "Biden"], and shouts "PLANESWALKERS / SPEARS" via Shatter + Mage Hand (Persuasion 28) to warn them, reducing planeswalker casualties. Ogre recognizes some planeswalkers, including [[Isiah Ja-Faank|Isaiah]].

**Operation Celery (Part 2):** Team Celery reaches the [[Selesnya Conclave]] and finds [[Nissa]] smoking with Saskia and Myra ("the bimbos from the factory"). Nissa is at her "operational" baseline of baked (Rin Insight 22); the Selesnya medical tents are organized and ready. Nissa has no firm word on [[Chandra]] or [[Gideon]] (last known on [[Arcavios]]/Strixhaven; Chandra lost first, Gideon went after her and vanished). She has seen "the Johnny" ([[Jace]]?) heading to the front. She praises [[Gideon]] as effectively unkillable ("Gideon the Invincible").

**The Pyramid & the Spark Harvest (Part 2):** An earth-shaking event raises a massive **pyramid citadel** from Plaza South with a bright white light at its peak. History checks (Rin 21, Sanis ~19, Ogre 7, Varis nat 1) connect the white light to the energy that left [[Jace]]'s body when the [[Guildpact]] shattered. Rin's Arcana 24 identifies the traveling white lights as **planeswalker sparks** being pulled toward and gathered at the pyramid. Rin realizes, devastated, that trapping the planeswalkers on Ravnica was the enemy's goal — the party "played right into their trap." (Orphie notes it was Ral, not the party, who trapped them.)

**Climax — God-Eternal Oketra (Part 2):** A 60-ft hulking archer — later identified by [[Samut]] as **God-Eternal [[God-Eternal Oketra|Oketra]]**, who "never misses" — emerges and takes aim. [[Sanis]] fires a flame arrow at extreme range with disadvantage (dirty 20 + 9 to hit; D8+D6 = 16, with [[Ugin]]'s white flame), striking Oketra's shoulder — a major personal moment ([[Varis]] tears up). This diverts Oketra's aim to [[Ogre's Tavern]]. Rin uncorks the ever-smoking bottle (60-ft cloud) and Orphie takes evasive maneuvers with [[Scrappy]], but Oketra's arrow (roll w/ disadvantage = 18) tears a hole through the tavern: **78 piercing damage** (Dex save for half). Orphie is the exception (grazed); Rin drops to **1 HP**; Sanis is struck. Orphie rages, gathers Sanis and orders Rin to fly, then uses **Blazebringer** to detach the tavern from Scrappy and lets it fall; Oketra's second arrow destroys the falling tavern outright. Sanis casts **Cure Wounds** on Rin mid-fall (2d8+4 = 15). The party nosedives blind through the smoke toward an unknown landing.

**Cliffhanger:** [[Samut]] (an [[Amonkhet]] ally from a prior one-shot) warns [[Varis]] to flee — Oketra never misses — as Oketra's gaze turns back toward Team Ogre-Sized. The DM ends the session there. The party's emotional note: they're alive, together, and the tavern is replaceable.

### Themes & Emotional Beats

- **Consent & Accountability:** Orphie's punch on Ral literalizes a lesson about consent — Ral summoned every planeswalker without asking, and Orphie demonstrates the violation physically. A recurring Orphie value: bodily autonomy and calling out abuse of power.
- **Found Family Over Possessions:** The session's title beat. Orphie sacrifices the beloved [[Ogre's Tavern]] without hesitation to save her friends — "I can't rebuild you." The party's bond is reaffirmed as they fall together.
- **The Trap Springs:** The planeswalker summoning, framed as triumph in Session 01, is revealed as the enemy's objective — gathering sparks to harvest. The party's "victory" set the table for catastrophe.
- **Chaos and Consequence:** Ogre's impulsive LDGB fusion (an accident) and Steve's underground enlargement (possible infrastructure damage) continue the pattern of unplanned, large-scale side effects from the party's actions. The silenced Dimir network is a direct consequence of Session 01.
- **Sanis's Heroism & Near-Death:** Sanis's reunification with Szadek pays off both mechanically (power-up) and dramatically (the impossible flame-arrow shot, witnessed with pride by Varis), immediately followed by his near-death in the tavern — the cost of drawing a god's attention.
- **Levity Amid Catastrophe:** The recumbent-bike tangent, the LGBTQIA+ "LDGBTQIA+" bit, and Nissa getting baked while the city burns underscore the table's tonal range and the characters' coping mechanisms.
