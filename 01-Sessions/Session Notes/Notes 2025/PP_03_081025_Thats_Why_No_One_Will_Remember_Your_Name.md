## Section 2 — Orphie's POV Overview

### THAT'S WHY NO ONE WILL REMEMBER YOUR NAME: ORPHIE'S ACCOUNT

We woke up to the whole world screaming. Not figuratively. The city was tearing itself apart on every side — one army crashing down from the north, a dead tide rising up from the south — and there was no version of the morning where we got to stay in bed. So we split. Ogre and Varis went where the fighting was loudest. Rin, Sanis and I went the other way, and then everything I'd thrown us out of the night before came roaring back, and a thing the size of a building put an arrow through the sky and swatted us down like we were nothing.

When I came to, my first job was the only job I ever really trust myself with: I got everyone off the wreck, made them stop, made them check. Everybody, all limbs attached. We've been doing this long enough that I know the panic comes later. First you count heads. Sanis had cookies, of all things, hoarded like a squirrel. We laughed. It had been so long since anything was that small and that kind.

Then the sky split open over the dead district and a dragon the size of a nightmare walked out and announced he'd come to judge us all. I want it on record that I am very tired of men deciding they get to be the one who decides. My parents were like that. This one was worse, and bigger, and he had a voice that got into your teeth.

The thing about being me, in a fight like this, is that I am exactly one woman with an axe. Everyone around me was throwing fire and turning into monsters and I kept asking — beg me to be bigger, give me a reason to be close enough to matter — and nobody could. So I drove. I put my people on the back of a half-dead mechanical horse and I cut a road through the dead so the ones who could do the impossible would get their chance. That's a kind of useful. It's just not the kind that feels like anything.

Chandra came back. Out of nowhere, out of years, thinner and harder and burning at the edges, and I called her a name and meant it like a hug. Of course it was her. It's always the people you stopped expecting.

And then — finally — the god went down. They wore it out, all of them at once, pinned it and burned it and wrapped it in everything we had, and when it hit the ground at our level I climbed up its leg and I carved my name into it. Small. So it'd know. So somebody would. I've spent this whole stretch feeling like furniture in my own story and I was not going to let a god die without leaving a mark that said I was here.

The other dragon, the one who actually liked us — they flew up to fight the judge, and they lost. I watched a thing that called this plane its own get folded into a black nothing and come out as bones. And they sent Varis a message on the way out telling us not to come. Don't be reckless. Wait. I'm so sick of dragons sacrificing themselves and telling us it's fine. It is never fine. I told Molly that no one would remember her name for falling back, and I knew even as I said it that I was really talking about all of us — about whether any of this leaves a scratch on the world or just gets swallowed like the rest.

I wanted to quit. I said something ugly about existence and Rin caught it before it could land, the way she does. And then something flicked me right between the eyes, hard, the way only one person ever did, and I heard her plain as day: survive, you idiot. So I will. Grandmothers don't ask twice.

We carried Chandra to the tree where the healers are. She'd been caged and starved and clawed for years for the crime of trying to save children, and she still tried to wave us off like it was nothing. She told us how they're stealing the spark out of people — how they kill them first and pull the light out after, with a spell and a spear. And just as we were catching our breath, a nervous girl with daggers and mushrooms in her hair pressed a bone into Sanis's hand and said his sister is hurt and waiting. So. No rest. There's never any rest. But I'm still standing, and I've got a name carved into a god to prove I was here, and that'll have to be enough for tonight.

## Section 3 — Session Analysis

### Narrative Summary

Session 03 is a single continuous combat-and-aftermath arc: the **Siege of Ravnica's 10th District**, picking up moments after Session 02 ended. It covers the party's battle against the invading [[Eternals]] and the **[[God-Eternal Oketra]]**, the arrival of **[[Nicol Bolas]]**, the death of **[[Niv-Mizzet]]**, the return of long-lost planeswalker **[[Chandra Nalaar]]**, and a regroup at the [[Selesnya Conclave]] that surfaces critical intel about the spark-harvesting weapon.

**Recap / Cold Open (Session 02 events).** The DM recaps: after destroying the [[Ghost Mind]] and absorbing its souls into Ogre's [[Leather Daddy Ball]], [[Ral Zarek|Ral]] activated the [[Interplanar Beacon]] by flipping his daily switch (now unblocked), summoning all planeswalkers to [[Ravnica]] and trapping them via the [[Immortal Sun]]. The party spoke with [[Dahlia]]. Ogre fused **[[Pip]]** (Pippi) with the Leather Daddy Ball, creating the **"Leather Daddy Game Boy"**; Pip is still recovering from the merge. After a long rest at the apartment, the party woke to citywide battle — [[Gruul Clans|Gruul]] attacking [[Boros Legion|Boros]] from the north, Eternals surging from the south ([[Golgari Swarm|Golgari]] and [[Simic Combine|Simic]] districts). The party split: **Ogre + Varis** to the front lines; **Orphie, Rin, Sanis** to the [[Selesnya Conclave|Celestial/Selesnya Conclave]] (met [[Nissa]], Myra, Saskia). They took the traveling Tavern to the battlefield; Varis turned dragon. **[[God-Eternal Oketra]]** appeared with a massive bow and shot the Tavern out of the sky. **Orphie threw everyone onto [[Scrappy]]** and detached the doomed Tavern, saving the party. Varis was warned that the God-Eternal is from Amonkhet — Oketra — and extremely powerful, and that the Eternals carry spark-harvesting staves.

**Opening Position.** Ogre and Varis are on the front lines pushing the Eternals back, but Oketra's arrival has **renewed Eternal morale**. Orphie, Rin, and Sanis are on the ground with Scrappy, behind enemy lines, in a quiet pocket of the DMZ — roughly **north of the 10th District Plaza, on the Boros/Orzhov border**. Oketra (a 60-foot zombie-god in white armor with a massive bow) is ~120 ft from the trio; Ogre and Varis are ~120 ft in the other direction. Oketra has turned its bow toward the front lines and is ignoring the trio.

**Triage & the Snickerdoodles.** The party heals before moving. Sanis eats one of three hoarded **freeze-dried Snickerdoodle cookies** (from "Murphy's mom" / Orphie's mom) for ~7–10 temp HP; the others roll group healing (3d4+3). Rin casts **Mass Healing Word** at 4th level (~15 HP to all). Orphie takes command of Scrappy to ferry the trio toward Ogre and Varis.

**Nicol Bolas Arrives.** From **[[Bolas's Citadel]]** — a massive black pyramid that rises out of the Golgari district — **[[Nicol Bolas]]** the elder dragon (~80 ft) emerges from a portal and walks onto Ravnica, proclaiming: *"It is time for my judgment for the plane of Ravnica. I am here and I will be the decision maker."* He retreats to the top of his citadel.

**The Battle for the Gaps.** In loose initiative, Oketra fires three arrows that break the Boros/[[Izzet League|Izzet]] line in three places. The party plugs all three: **Rin** casts **Wall of Fire** (60 ft long, ~20 ft high), incinerating 14 Eternals at one gap; **Sanis** rains Longbow fire (including **Hail of Thorns**, shredding 5 Eternals around an undead sorcerer, and Varis-enchanted fire arrows) to thin a second; **Orphie** leaps into the third and **cleaves** through Eternals and undead jackals with **[[Blazebringer]]**.

**Ogre's Shadow-of-the-Colossus Gambit.** Ogre **misty steps onto [[Steve]]** (the giant Golgari rotworm, doubled in size by "embiggening mushrooms," eating Eternals), then has **Varis fly him like a missile** into a crevice in Oketra's armor. Ogre pops a **shrinking mushroom** to go tiny (~6–8 in), squeezes between armor and skin (Con save DC 16, succeeds), and **lowers Oketra's AC by 2**; Varis **pops off Oketra's shoulder pauldron**, leaving the god more exposed. Ogre then burrows toward the chest, hunting the heart, finding the "lazotep-plated" skin nearly impossible to pierce.

**Chandra Returns.** A small figure runs to Oketra's feet — revealed to be **[[Chandra Nalaar]]** (thin, haggard, amber-red flaming hair), absent for roughly **seven years**. She immediately starts hurling **Fireballs** at Oketra's face. The party is stunned and overjoyed.

**Molly & the Skyknights.** **[[Molly]]** arrives commanding **[[Boros Legion|Boros]] Skyknight Legionnaires** on griffins: *"Carve a path to the God-Eternal."* Rin regains her nerve, casts **Celestial Revelation** (sprouts wings), joins the skyknights, and Fireballs a clear lane. Orphie drives Scrappy up the lane with Sanis shooting over her shoulder, clearing jackals and sorcerers.

**Dragon vs. Dragon.** **[[Niv-Mizzet]]** (the [[Izzet League]] guildleader, they/them, the "Fire-Mind") flies across the battlefield toward Bolas, winking/nodding at Varis in passing: *"This is my plane and you will not meddle in our affairs so easily."* A dragon duel begins atop the citadel — Niv-Mizzet hurling lightning and tail-slung skyscrapers, trading claw strikes with Bolas. The party celebrates the prospect of Niv-Mizzet possibly killing Ral.

**Killing a God.** The party coordinates a takedown of the now-engaged Oketra: **Varis** multi-attacks (bite + claws), uses **frightful presence** (dragon roar) to scatter lesser Eternals, lands a **breath weapon for 64 force damage**, then **wing-attacks Oketra prone** and **grapples** it (Athletics 25 vs 21), pinning its shoulders. **Steve** wraps its legs → **restrained**. **Orphie** climbs the downed god and **carves her name into its thigh** with Blazebringer. **Chandra** casts **Immolation** (35 fire); **Sanis** hacks with a magicked shortsword; **Rin** burns it with Fireball/Scorching Ray; Skyknights hurl spears. Finally **Ogre**, inside the chest cavity, **stabs the heart** twice with his soul-weapon (transcript "**Witch**"; DDB logs the weapon as "Duskblade") for psychic damage, and on a Constitution save begins **drawing Oketra's soul through the weapon**. The 60-foot God-Eternal shivers, goes still, and its **energy/soul is harvested** away. **Oketra is dead.**

**Niv-Mizzet Falls.** In the back, Bolas gazes into Niv-Mizzet's soul (a first flicker of fear on the Fire-Mind), conjures a huge dark sphere — *"This is the end for you, Fire-Mind"* — and **engulfs and disintegrates Niv-Mizzet**, leaving only a skeleton that crumples to the ground. The body is too destroyed to revivify.

**The Choice to Fall Back.** Molly urges retreat; the party wants to charge in to save Niv-Mizzet, but **Varis receives a telepathic message from Niv**: *"You'll know what to do… just let this happen, and wait."* Sanis (Insight 20) reads that Niv had a plan and wouldn't have charged Bolas recklessly. The party stands down. Bolas returns to a **meditative pose atop his citadel, "aura farming"** — visibly **absorbing planeswalker sparks** (orbs rising from across the 10th District). Chandra (Arcana, dirty 20) confirms the sparks are harvested from planeswalkers and fed to Bolas.

**Regroup with Molly.** The Izzet bring **armored vehicles ("tanks")** and the Selesnya heal the wounded; the line holds despite an unending undead flow. The party resolves to find **where the Eternal army is entering** (the Golgari Guild; undead sorcerers are from **[[Strixhaven]]**) and to **stop the spark harvesting**. Threads raised: re-establish contact with the isolated **[[Rakdos]]** district; the fates of **[[Kaya Cassir|Kaya]]**, **Ral**, and **Dahlia**.

**Flight to Vitu-Ghazi & Chandra's Story.** The party flies (on dragon-Varis) to the **[[Selesnya Conclave]]** at the Great Tree of **[[Vitu-Ghazi]]**. **[[Nissa]]** intakes Chandra in tears. There is nothing to be done for Chandra's **black spiral scarring** (a talon-raked mark on her left arm, related to but not identical to marks seen on **[[Penny]]** and **[[Selena]]**) — she needs rest, food, and friends. Chandra reveals: she infiltrated **Strixhaven**, found the school's students were being abducted and **turned into undead** for their sparks, and founded a rebellion — **the Flameseekers of Truth**. She was **betrayed by someone named [[Onyx]]**, captured, and imprisoned/tortured for years.

**The Elder Spell & Brula.** Chandra explains the harvesting method: the **Elder Spell**, cast in tandem with a **spark-harvester trident**, extracts planeswalker sparks (victims die first; the spark is then removed). A nervous elf woman with two daggers — **[[Brula]]**, revealed via Insight/Arcana to be a **were-zombie like Selena** — enters and presses a **bone sprouting purple mushrooms** into Sanis's hand, reporting that **[[Selena]]** (Sanis's sister) was hurt at the **[[Bone Mine]]** and is recovering there. The mushroom-bone magic resembles the Strixhaven magic (but not the Elder Spell) and piques Chandra's curiosity.

**Closing.** The party plans to drop **Scrappy** for repair (Izzet Guild / Golgari with Dahlia) and follow Brula to Selena at the Bone Mine. As the session ends, Varis (Arcana 17) feels a part of his stowed belongings **grow warm** — *"It's the box!"* — and the session cuts there.

### Themes & Emotional Beats

- **Smallness in a War of Titans:** Orphie's recurring lament — *"I just want to be big"* — anchors the session emotionally. Surrounded by dragons, gods, and spellcasters, the party's lone melee fighter spends most of the battle as a driver and lane-clearer, and her name-carving on the felled god is a deliberate, defiant claim to having mattered.
- **Legacy & Being Remembered:** Orphie's barb to Molly — *"that's why no one will remember your name"* — and her wider despair ("existence is hell") frame a session about whether anything they do leaves a mark, against a villain who literally erases people into bone.
- **Sacrificial Dragons:** Niv-Mizzet's death echoes Ugin's earlier self-sacrifice. The party is openly exhausted by powerful beings telling them to "let it happen and wait."
- **Reunion & Survivors' Guilt:** Chandra's return after seven years of torture, and her insistence that she's "fine," lands as both joy and grief. Her rebellion (Flameseekers of Truth) and betrayal (Onyx) seed a personal arc.
- **The Machinery of the Spark War:** The Elder Spell + spark-harvester trident make the multiversal threat concrete and stoppable — the party now has a mechanism to fight, not just a god to fear.
- **Found Family Under Fire:** The Capri-Sun/Snickerdoodle "shawarma scene" banter, the protective triage, and carrying Chandra to safety reaffirm the Breakfast Club's core bond amid catastrophe.

Expect arcs around (a) protecting potential victims pre-extraction and (b) destroying the harvester staves/tridents — Ogre is already "trying to find those spears to break them."

**"The Box" Stinger:** Varis's stowed "box" growing warm is an unresolved cliffhanger — track for next session.
