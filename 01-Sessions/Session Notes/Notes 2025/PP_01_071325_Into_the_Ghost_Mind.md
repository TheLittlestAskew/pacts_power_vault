## Section 2 — Orphie's POV Overview

### THE DEVIL YOU KNOW: ORPHIES'S ACCOUNT

We stood at the top of the staircase, staring into nothing. Not darkness—nothing. The kind of dark that swallows torchlight whole. [[Ogre]] was already halfway down before any of us could say anything. I lit a torch with my tail and realized pretty quickly that fire doesn't mean much when magic is eating the light. I shouted at him that we couldn't see. He didn't care.

[[Sanis]] tried to break whatever was holding the dark in place. His magic reached out and something reached back and pinched it shut. That was new. That was wrong. Whatever lived down here wasn't just powerful—it was aware. And it was watching us fumble in the black like children. [[Rin]] figured out the energy came from deeper in, something related to but not quite the same as the Ghost Mind's own power. So we held hands and let Ogre lead us. I hated every second of it. But I didn't have a better idea.

At the bottom we found a door in the floor. Ogre tried to pull it open while standing on it. I love him, but Azoreth preserve us. I opened it with one hand. Beyond it, a chamber with four urns. A voice told us these were traitors, and we were to judge them—free them or burn their essences. Just like that. No trial, no evidence, just a voice in the dark telling us to play executioner.

I didn't like it. None of us did. We listened to each spirit's story. One blew a conduit to save a city and hundreds died. One sold secrets to survive. One tried to kill a guildmaster to stop a war. One committed the unforgivable sin of questioning the system. Every single one of them made a choice that cost them everything—and every single one of them had a reason I could understand, even if I didn't agree. We freed them all. What right did we have to judge the dead when we can barely keep the living sorted?

The third and fourth urns hit us hard. Some kind of psychic blast when we cracked them open. [[Varis]] took the worst of it—I saw him stagger. But he held. We all held. That's what we do.

Then came the choir. Four ghosts singing about [[Szadek]]—his rise, his fall, the way the [[House Dimir|Dimir]] twisted his legacy into something he never intended. Ogre tried to brute-force it and got psychic feedback for his trouble. Sanis knew the history. He conducted them into the right order, and the song resolved into something that almost sounded like mourning.

After that, a staircase made of glass. Ten steps, each one a statement about our world, about us. True or false. Step on the wrong answer and it hurts. We got nine out of ten. Rin stepped wrong on one about the [[Guildpact]] being dead—turns out it's not, technically. The ley lines are still intact. The thing that holds this whole plane together is still there, buried under everything that went wrong. That felt important. That felt like something worth remembering.

At the bottom, a throne. And on it, the [[Ghost Mind]] wearing Szadek's face. Not Szadek himself—a construct, a copy, a thing that had consumed so many ghosts it had become something else entirely. It spoke to us. It tested us. And then Ogre did what Ogre does—he pulled out Witch and started swinging. Every wandering spirit in the place got sucked into the [[Leather Daddy Ball]]. Just—gone. Centuries of accumulated intelligence, centuries of Dimir secrets, all vacuumed up like dust.

Sanis stepped forward and absorbed the Szadek fragment. Took it into himself, into that mind palace where Szadek already lived. For the first time, Szadek was whole. I watched Sanis's face when it happened and I couldn't tell if he was relieved or terrified. Maybe both.

The Ghost Mind collapsed. We barely made it out. Outside, [[Kaya Cassir]] was waiting—the planeswalker ghost-killer who'd been tethered to Sanis. She looked at what we'd done, said we'd done her job for her, and cut the tether. Just like that. Gone.

Then the world shifted. One second we were standing in Dimir territory, the next we were on top of the [[Fortress of Nivix]], the Izzet Guild's tower. [[Ral Zarek]] was there—married now, apparently, to someone named [[Tomik]]—and he'd just activated the [[Interplanar Beacon]]. The thing that calls every planeswalker in the multiverse to one spot. He'd been trying for weeks, but the Ghost Mind's interference had been blocking it. We removed the interference.

Hundreds of planeswalkers, arriving on [[Ravnica]]. And then—the [[Immortal Sun]]. Ogre felt it first. That wall. The one that says you can't leave. Every planeswalker summoned is now trapped here. Including us. Including whatever's coming.

Three guilds aren't aligned: [[Golgari Swarm|Golgari]], [[Simic Combine|Simic]], and [[Gruul Clans|Gruul]]. [[Borborygmos]] got overthrown. The ley lines are intact but nobody's holding them together. And somewhere in the Golgari tunnels, something is opening a bridge from another world.

I don't know what's coming. But I know it's big, and I know we're not ready.

## Section 3 — Session Analysis

### Narrative Summary

The session begins at the entrance staircase descending into the [[Ghost Mind]], a massive arcane construct located in [[House Dimir|Dimir]] territory on [[Ravnica]]. The Ghost Mind is a cylindrical column housing centuries of accumulated ghostly intelligence—effectively the Dimir Guild's information network and archive. The party previously completed entrance rituals in a prior (short) session.

**The Descent (Magical Darkness):** The party descends the staircase and immediately encounters magical darkness that nullifies all light sources and resists [[Sanis]]'s Dispel Magic (rolled 18, insufficient). [[Ogre]] can see through it due to his warlock abilities. [[Rin]] determines via Arcana (23) that the darkness originates from deeper in the Ghost Mind and is related to but distinct from the Ghost Mind's own power. The party holds hands with Ogre guiding via mage hands. Along the way, Ogre swipes at wandering ghost souls (d20: 16, collecting 8).

**The Urn Chamber — Judgment Puzzle:** At the bottom, the party finds a chamber with four urns containing condemned spirits. An automated voice instructs them to judge each: free or burn. The party investigates the urns (multiple Investigation checks: [[Orphie]] 18, Ogre 12, [[Varis]] 11, Rin 12). Ogre identifies the spirits via History (15). Each urn triggers a spirit testimony:
- Urn 1: Blew a conduit to save a city; hundreds died
- Urn 2: Sold secrets to survive
- Urn 3: Attempted to kill a guildmaster to prevent a war
- Urn 4: Questioned the system

The party frees all four. Urns 3 and 4 trigger Constitution saves dealing psychic damage (5 on pass, 10 on fail). Varis takes the worst hit (Con save: 10, failed, 10 psychic damage).

**The Ghost Choir:** Four spectral singers perform a song about [[Szadek]]'s history. Ogre attempts to interact directly and takes 10 psychic damage. Sanis identifies the historical events (History: dirty 20) and conducts the choir in chronological order (Performance: 13). The choir resolves.

**The Glass Staircase of Truth:** Ten transparent steps, each bearing a lore statement (true or false). Stepping on the correct answer is safe; the wrong answer deals psychic damage. The party navigates with one mistake—Rin takes 5 psychic damage on "The Guildpact is dead" (answer: False—the ley lines are intact). Key revelations from the staircase:
- [[Kaya Cassir]] killed [[Szadek]] and became Obzedat's enforcer
- [[Szadek]] had memory gaps; was not fully himself
- [[Ogre]] was once arrested by the [[Azorius Senate]]
- The [[Guildpact]] ley lines are intact and potentially restorable
- The Levistus family lineage is NOT descended from magic (confirmed false)

**The Ghost Mind Throne:** At the bottom, the Ghost Mind sits on a throne wearing Szadek's form. Ogre uses Witch to exorcise all wandering spirits in the structure—they are absorbed into the [[Leather Daddy Ball]]. Sanis absorbs the Szadek fragment, reuniting Szadek in his mind palace. The Ghost Mind collapses.

**Kaya's Departure:** Outside the collapsed Ghost Mind, [[Kaya Cassir]] appears and voluntarily severs the tether connecting her to Sanis. She departs.

**Fortress of Nivix / Interplanar Beacon:** The party is involuntarily planeswalked to the top of the [[Fortress of Nivix]]. [[Ral Zarek]] is present; his daily attempts to activate the [[Interplanar Beacon]] have finally succeeded now that the Ghost Mind's interference is gone. Hundreds of planeswalkers are arriving on Ravnica. Ral reveals he is married to [[Tomik]] and possesses planeswalker-detecting glasses.

**Immortal Sun / Endgame Setup:** The [[Immortal Sun]] activates—all planeswalkers are now trapped on Ravnica. Ogre recognizes the feeling from a previous encounter on Ixalan. Three guilds remain unaligned: [[Golgari Swarm|Golgari]], [[Simic Combine|Simic]], and [[Gruul Clans|Gruul]] ([[Borborygmos]] has been overthrown). Ral departs on a lightning disc to gather allies. The party heads downstairs to find Dahlia.

**DM Stinger (Not Party Knowledge):** In the [[Golgari Swarm|Golgari]] territory, an interplanar bridge opens. Rows of embalmed [[Eternals]] from Amonkhet's necropolis emerge alongside god-sized coffins cracking open. [[The Second Sun]] / [[Nicol Bolas]]'s forces are invading Ravnica.

### Themes & Emotional Beats

- **Judgment Without Authority:** The urn puzzle forces the party to pass judgment on spirits whose crimes are morally complex. The party's decision to free all four reflects their growing resistance to systems that demand compliance without transparency.
- **The Cost of Knowledge:** Every puzzle in the Ghost Mind exacts a psychic toll. The deeper the party goes, the more they learn—and the more it hurts. The Glass Staircase literalizes this: truth has a price when you step wrong.
- **Ogre as Chaos Agent:** Ogre's impulsive ghost-killing solved the Ghost Mind overload but also destroyed the Dimir's entire intelligence network. This mirrors a recurring campaign pattern where his instinctive actions produce results nobody planned for.
- **Szadek's Reunification:** Szadek is now whole in Sanis's mind palace for the first time. The implications for Sanis's autonomy and power remain unclear.
- **Escalation — War of the Spark Setup:** The session's final act pivots from dungeon crawl to multiversal crisis. The Immortal Sun, the Interplanar Beacon, and the Eternals converge into an endgame scenario.
- **Orphie's Lineage:** The Glass Staircase confirms the Levistus family is NOT descended from magic. This is a significant identity revelation for Orphie.

**Ogre's Role as Chaos Agent:** Ogre's impulsive ghost-killing solved the Ghost Mind overload problem but also destroyed the Dimir's intelligence network. This mirrors a recurring campaign pattern where Ogre's instinctive actions produce results the party didn't plan for.

**Szadek Reunification:** Szadek is now whole in Sanis's mind palace for the first time. What this means for Sanis's power level, Szadek's agency, and the Dimir's future remains to be seen.

**The Immortal Sun:** This is the second time the party has encountered the Immortal Sun trapping planeswalkers. The first was on Ixalan. The fact that Ogre recognizes the feeling suggests prior campaign events will be critical context for what follows.

**War of the Spark Convergence:** The Interplanar Beacon, the Immortal Sun, and the Eternals are all now active simultaneously on Ravnica. This is the campaign's endgame setup. The three unaligned guilds (Golgari, Simic, Gruul) represent the political obstacles, while the Eternals represent the military threat.

**Orphie's Lineage:** The Glass Staircase confirmed the Levistus family is NOT descended from magic. This is a significant identity shift for Orphie and may influence her character arc going forward.
